Noun Gender

Italian nouns are either masculine or feminine.

  • Masculine: Usually end in -o (il ragazzo, il libro)
  • Feminine: Usually end in -a (la ragazza, la casa)
  • Exception: Nouns ending in -e can be either gender (il cane, la chiave)

Definite Articles (The)

Italian has different words for "the" depending on gender and what letter follows.

  • il: Masculine singular before consonants (il libro)
  • lo: Masculine singular before s+consonant, z, y, ps, gn (lo studente, lo zaino)
  • la: Feminine singular before consonants (la casa)
  • l': Before vowels for both genders (l'amico, l'amica)
  • i: Masculine plural for "il" (i libri)
  • gli: Masculine plural for "lo" (gli studenti)
  • le: Feminine plural (le case)

Subject Pronouns

Italian subject pronouns (often optional because verbs show who's speaking):

  • io = I
  • tu = you (informal singular)
  • lui/lei = he/she
  • Lei = you (formal singular - capitalized!)
  • noi = we
  • voi = you (plural)
  • loro = they
Note: Italians often drop pronouns!
"Parlo italiano" (I speak Italian) - "io" is implied
